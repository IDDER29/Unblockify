How it works

From "I'm stuck" to moving again — in minutes

  1. 1
    Student reports a blockage

    They describe what they're stuck on, what they've already tried, and where they are in the curriculum. Optionally anonymous.

  2. 2
    AI Teaching Assistant responds immediately

    Within seconds, a Socratic response arrives — grounded in the cohort brief and past resolutions for similar blockages. It asks the right questions to get the student thinking again.

  3. 3
    Student unblocks themselves (or escalates)

    If the AI response does the job, the student marks "This unblocked me" — no human needed, full credit to the intervention. If not, they keep the thread open and ask follow-up questions.

  4. 4
    Instructor sees it in their queue

    Unclaimed blockages surface in the instructor queue, ordered by time open. The instructor claims it, reads the thread, and resolves — with an AI-drafted reply to start from if they want it.

  5. 5
    Data compounds over time

    Every resolved blockage feeds the knowledge base. Analytics show the owner which topics stall students, which instructors resolve fastest, and which students are at risk of dropping off.
